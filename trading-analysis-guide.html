The Foundations of Technical Analysis

This section covers the foundational philosophies that underpin all of technical analysis. Understanding these core tenets is essential for interpreting chart data correctly.

The Three Pillars of Technical Doctrine

  • The Market Discounts Everything: Price reflects all currently known information.
  • Prices Move in Trends: Trends can persist, and trading with trend direction improves context.
  • History Tends to Repeat Itself: Human behavior frequently creates recurring patterns.

Technical vs. Fundamental Analysis

Technical analysis focuses on price and volume behavior, while fundamental analysis assesses intrinsic value. Many traders use both: fundamentals for asset selection and technicals for timing.

Strategy Checklists

High-Momentum Trend-Following

Enter established trends on pullback confirmation.

  • Trend: 50 EMA above 200 EMA.
  • Entry Zone: Pullback to 50 EMA area.
  • Trigger: MACD line crossing above signal line.
  • Confirmation: Supporting volume expansion.

Mean-Reversion at Extremes

Trade reversals from stretched conditions.

  • Extreme: RSI < 30 or RSI > 70.
  • Confirmation: Outer Bollinger Band interaction.
  • Trigger: Reversal candle confirmation.
  • Target: Mid-band mean reversion zone.
